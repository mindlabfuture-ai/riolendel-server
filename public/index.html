Step by step

Take care of the gold you already own

01

Clean it gently

Warm water, a drop of mild soap, and a soft-bristle brush is all you need. Dry immediately with a soft, lint-free cloth — don't let pieces air-dry with soap residue still on them.

02

Store pieces separately

Soft pouches or a lined box keep pieces from scratching each other. Gold is softer than most metals people expect — tossing pieces in one drawer together causes more damage than most accidents do.

03

Take it off before...

Swimming, showering, exercising, or cleaning the house. Chlorine and many household chemicals weaken gold alloys over repeated exposure — this is the single most common cause of long-term damage.

04

Document it while things are calm

Photograph each piece and keep receipts or appraisals together, separate from the jewelry itself. This matters far more than people expect — for insurance, resale, and passing pieces down.

05

If you ever decide to sell

Get two or three quotes before accepting one. Resale value usually reflects melt/scrap value — the craftsmanship you originally paid for is rarely recovered. Our calculator above gives you a starting reference point.

Quick reference

Do this, not that

Do: mild soap, soft brush, lint-free cloth, separate storage.

Avoid: chlorine/bleach, ultrasonic cleaners on gemstone pieces, abrasive scrubbing on high-karat gold, wearing during swimming or exercise.

Most damage isn't an accident — it's a habit nobody thought twice about.

Two ways to hold the same metal

Physical gold and digital gold aren't rivals

Most Filipino households built their gold holdings the traditional way. Digital gold is a newer format for the same underlying asset — not a replacement, a different tool for a different job.

Physical gold — jewelry & bars

  • You can wear it, gift it, and pass it down — it carries family meaning
  • No app, no internet, no third party holding your asset
  • Selling means finding a buyer, and jewelry resale rarely returns full gold value — craftsmanship isn't refunded
  • Storage and safety are on you — a home safe or a bank box
  • Hard to buy or sell in small amounts, like ₱500 at a time

Digital gold — apps like GCash, Maya

  • Buy or sell in small pesos, any hour, from your phone
  • Price tracks the gold spot price directly — no jewelry markup or making charges
  • You're trusting an app and its gold custodian, not holding metal yourself
  • Newer in the Philippines — track record is still short
  • Nothing to wear, gift, or hand down as an heirloom
Plain-language walkthrough

What "digital gold" actually means

01

A provider holds real, physical gold

Regulated gold-backed products are meant to be backed 1:1 by bullion sitting in a vault — you're not buying a promise, you're buying a claim on stored metal.

02

Your app shows you a fraction of an ounce

Instead of buying a whole bar, you buy however many pesos you want — the app converts that into grams or fractions of an ounce at the live spot price.

03

The price moves with the real gold market

What you see in the app should track the same international spot price shown in our ticker above — not a rate the app sets on its own.

04

You can convert back to pesos, usually instantly

Selling is typically same-day, unlike physical gold where you need a willing buyer and may lose value to resale discounts.

Before you put money anywhere

Three questions worth asking any gold app

1. Is it backed by real, audited bullion — or just a number on a screen?

2. Is the provider registered with Philippine regulators (SEC and/or BSP)?

3. Can you actually withdraw your pesos back out, and how fast?

If a platform can't answer these clearly, that's your answer.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Is the jewelry in your shop real gold?

Yes — it's genuine 18K gold, but made using a lightweight "pawnable" (hollow/ampaw) construction, which is why it's priced lower than solid pieces of the same size. That's normal for this category, not a sign it's fake. If you're ever unsure, most Metro Manila jewelers and pawnshops can test purity on the spot.

Is digital gold better than physical gold?

It's not a competition — they serve different purposes. Physical gold carries family meaning and you hold it yourself; digital gold is flexible and quick to convert. Many Filipino households hold both rather than choosing just one.

How do I know if a gold investment platform is a scam?

The clearest warning sign is a promise of a fixed or guaranteed return — real gold prices move up and down, so nothing about holding gold is guaranteed. Also verify the platform is backed by real, audited bullion and check its SEC/BSP registration before funding an account.

How much is my gold jewelry worth in pesos today?

Use the free gold value calculator above — enter the weight in grams and karat of any piece to estimate its melt value at today's live gold price. Actual resale offers from buyers are typically at or below this melt value.

What karat is most Philippine gold jewelry?

Most gold jewelry sold in the Philippines is 18K (75% pure gold), though 21K, 22K, and 24K pieces are also common, especially for bars and coins.
